Introduction

Infection prevention and control (IPAC) means stopping germs from spreading (Public Health Ontario, 2023). It involves daily practices like washing hands, wearing protective gear, cleaning surfaces, and following rules to protect patients, staff, and the public.

Our Goal

We want fewer infections and a healthy community. By understanding the chain of infection (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 2022), we can break it through hand hygiene, PPE, cleaning, and isolation.

Importance of PPE

PPE keeps healthcare workers and patients safe (Ontario Ministry of Health, 2021). A basic kit includes gloves, masks, eye protection, gowns, and respirators.

Transmission & Precaution

There are three main transmission types: contact, droplet, and airborne (CDC, 2022). Standard precautions apply to all patients; transmission-based precautions depend on the infection type.
